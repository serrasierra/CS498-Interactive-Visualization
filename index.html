Evolution of Fuel Efficiency in Cars

Since the birth of the modern car in 1886, humans have have relied mostly on gasoline to fuel their cars. Throughtout most of the 1900s, there wasn't much advocacy for better fuel efficiency in cars. It wasn't until 1975, when congress passed the Energy Policy and Conservation Act of 1975, which established fuel economy standards for cars starting with the model year 1978. By 1985 cars would be expected to have doubled their fuel efficiency to a respectable 27.5 MPG.
